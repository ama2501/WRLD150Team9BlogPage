Inter/Cultural Events & Activities - Online


Cultural Connections: The Key to Intercultural Communication

Enhancing intercultural communication is important for fostering understanding and empathy across diverse cultures. Through effective communication, we can bridge cultural divides and navigate differences more constructively. Furthermore, embracing cultural knowledge enriches perspectives, promotes inclusivity, and strengthens global interconnectedness, creating a more harmonious world.

Experience Global Cultures Virtually

Accessing intercultural events online is not only convenient, but it allows you to join from anywhere in the world, saving travel time. Moreover, these events expand your cultural knowledge by exposing you to diverse audiences. Virtual networking facilitates meaningful connections and exchanges, expanding your cultural understanding. Embracing these opportunities not only broadens your cultural horizons but also enhances your global awareness and empathy, equipping you with valuable skills for navigating an increasingly interconnected world.

This blog highlights cultural events and activities suited for university students. Each event is selected for its affordability and easy accessibility, offering a blend of online talks and local fairs. Additionally, these events provide enriching learning experiences and opportunities to connect with new cultures and people. Discover and enjoy different cultures right from your campus or home!

Eating Up Easter: Film & Discussion

Join us for a screening of Sergio Mata’u Rapu and Elena Kouneski Rapu film documentary Eating up Easter. Set in Rapa Nui (Easter Island, Chile), this film explores the conflict between indigenous populations and local communities as they both work towards economic development and environmental sustainability. Don't miss this thought-provoking documentary that displays the delicate balance between progress and preservation in one of the world's most remote and beautiful places! After the screening, make sure to stick around for a captivating discussion where we'll delve deeper into the themes of sustainability, cultural preservation, and community resilience portrayed in the film.

  • Duration: 2hrs
  • Price: Donation
  • Date: June 24, 2024 5:00pm-7:00pm PDT
